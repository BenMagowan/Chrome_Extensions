# Privacy Policy

**Effective Date:** July 16, 2026

This Privacy Policy describes how we handle information when you install and use our Chrome Extension.

We are committed to protecting your privacy. The short version is: **this extension does not collect, store, transmit, or share any of your personal data.**

## 1. Data Collection and Usage

**We do not collect any personal data.**

To function, the extension requires access to the active webpage when you are playing games on the LinkedIn platform (such as Queens, Tango, Mini Sudoku, Zip, or Patches).

The extension operates **entirely locally** on your device. When you use the extension:

* It reads the structure of the current game state (such as grid dimensions, puzzle elements, words, or cell states) directly from your browser's Document Object Model (DOM).
* It computes the solution using algorithms running locally on your machine.
* It simulates clicks or keystrokes to interact with the game board.

None of this game data, nor any information about your LinkedIn profile, browsing history, or account, is ever transmitted to external servers, developers, or third parties.

## 2. Chrome Web Store Permissions

To provide its core functionality, the extension requests the following permissions:

* **Host Permission:** This is required solely to read the puzzle data and interact with the game boards across LinkedIn's gaming directory. The extension cannot and does not access any other pages on LinkedIn (such as your feed, messages, network, or profile).
* **Scripting:** This allows the extension to inject the local solving algorithms directly into the game page environment.

## 3. Third-Party Services and Tracking

This extension does not use any third-party analytics, tracking pixels, telemetry, or advertising frameworks. Your usage of the extension remains completely private to your local machine.

## 4. Changes to This Privacy Policy

We may update this Privacy Policy from time to time to reflect changes in our practices or for other operational, legal, or regulatory reasons. Any changes will be updated on this page with a revised "Effective Date."
